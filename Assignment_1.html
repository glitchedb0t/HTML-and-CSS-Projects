(Some of) The Most Popular TTRPG Systems Ever

Dungeons and Dragons

First published in 1974

Designed originally by Gary Gygax and Dave Arneson and published by TSR. Currently on its fifth edition.

Games give you a chance to excel, and if you're playing in good company you don't even mind if you lose because you had the enjoyment of the company during the course of the game. - Gary Gygax

Cyberpunk

First published in 1988

Designed originally by Mike Pondsmith and pubhlished by R. Talsorian Games. Currently on its fourth edition.

Cyberpunk was a warning, not an aspiration - Mike Pondsmith

Call of Cthulhu

First published in 1981

Designed originally by Sandy Petersen and published by Chaosium. Currently on its seventh edition.

Here’s the deal. If you want a game in which you have the same old steroid-pumped champions confronting the baddies, every other RPG can provide this. But if you want a game in which the emphasis is far more cerebral, and more dangerous, and in which the enemies pose an existential threat – there is only Call of Cthulhu. - Sandy Petersen

Vampire: The Masquerade

First published in 1991

Designed originally by Mark Rein-Hagen, Graeme Davis, Tom Dowd, Lisa Stevens, and Stewart Wieck and published by White Wolf Publishing. Currently on its fifth edition.

If your imagination is superior to the rules, go beyond the rules. - Mark Rein-Hagen
